The Myth of Sisyphus

Created2022-10-25|Reading Notes
|Word count:533|Reading time:3min|Comments:

An Absurd Reasoning

I sort of love this analogy Camus uses to unveil the fact that we all actually live in an world filled with absurdity, especially given our life routine follows some systems may or may not designed by our will. In fact, we’re all Sisyphus, a seemingly pathetic character. One would inevitably begin to wonder the meaning of life - whether it is worth living at all as soon as he discovers the fact. Is it a soluble question?

I have long been yearning for the answer to this question since I was very young. When I was still a teenager, I interviewed around a hundred schoolmates asking what did they think of the meaning of life is. Interestingly, 70% of them replied: why don’t you just go die? The unspoken assumption I presume in their reply was: if you didn’t think there is one, or you didn’t have an answer to it already. It seems so nature that people would connect the meaning of life with death or suicide.

There is still this other half of people who try to entail a meaning to life. My mother, as an example, replied: well, it is to breed offsprings - that’s how you reproduce generations to generations. That’s…quite an answer.

Nowadays, as biological philosophy and gene theory prevails, it won’t be hard to imagine an answer be “people live because our gene tells us to”. However, a question it doesn’t answer is that why is our gene coded in a way that makes it possible for people to realize absurdity at all? Wouldn’t it be easier for them to pass along if such ideas as absurdity, suicide or even dink are never allowed?

It is the fact that one is capable of being aware of this absence of profound reason for living wonders me the most. As Camus says:

This divorce between man and his life, the actor and his setting, is properly the feeling of absurdity.

Therefore, here comes eluding, and a typical act of that is hope.

Hope of another life one must “deserve” or trickery of those who live not for life itself but for some great idea that will transcend it, refine it, give it a meaning, and betray it.

Religions, beliefs, and stories derived across all age to fulfill this hope.

It cannot be neglected that there is a small group, or could be majority, of people who believe in neither of the stories for hope nor the meaning of life.

..it must be said that of the thinkers who refused a meaning to life none…admitted his logic to the point of refusing that life.

A healthy and rational man must have questioned that or thought of suicide at some point in his life but somehow it went to a stop. I myself is getting closer to this type which I’d call a “walking dead”.

That’s probably a difference between philosophers and ordinary men: they never stop but keep bringing clarities to those obscures every man wonders.
